If you want the rainbow, you have to put up with the rain.

I've been fat for a while now. At my heaviest, I was around 270 lb. For someone who is 5'9" (5'10" if I'm being really forgiving to myself), I was decidedly way too short for my weight.

I can do the jovial fat guy thing as good as the next person, but I was seriously just tired of being heavy: to have clothing not fit well, to not have energy, and to generally not feel great.

After doing a bunch of coaching and diet programs that didn't work, I reached out to a physician to help me kickstart my weight loss. When I visited them this past September, I was at 254 lb. After having been placed on a super restrictive diet (1000 calories a day, high protein, no carbs) and an exercise regimen, the past few months have clearly sucked. _I miss food._

But on the upside, I am able to post for the first time in at least a decade that I am at a record low weight: 197.2 lb. I haven't seen the underside of 200 lb for as long as I can remember (at least 15 years), so this is kind of a big deal to me. I'd ideally like to get down to about 180 lb, but slow and steady wins the race.

Here's to hoping that I'm considerably more svelt on the next time that I post a weight update.
